Early Life:
Full Name: Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar
Born: April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India
Family Background: Tendulkar came from a modest family. His father, Ramesh Tendulkar, was a reputed writer, and his mother, Rajni Tendulkar, worked as a teacher.
Cricket Career:
Debut: Tendulkar made his international debut for India in Test cricket against Pakistan on November 15, 1989, and in One Day Internationals (ODIs) against the same team on December 18, 1989.
Records and Achievements:
Most Runs in International Cricket: Tendulkar holds the record for the most runs in both Test matches and ODIs.
Centuries: He is the only cricketer to have scored 100 international centuries (51 in Tests and 49 in ODIs).
World Cup: He was part of the Indian team that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.
First to Reach 10,000 ODI Runs: Tendulkar was the first player to cross the 10,000-run mark in ODIs.
200th Test Match: He was the first cricketer to play 200 Test matches.
Playing Style:
Batting: Tendulkar was known for his impeccable technique, elegance, and ability to adapt his game to all formats. His signature shots, including the straight drive and the cover drive, are celebrated for their precision and beauty.
Records: His consistency and longevity in the game earned him numerous records and accolades.
Post-Retirement:
Retirement: Tendulkar retired from international cricket in 2013. He was honored with a farewell match against the West Indies at his home ground, Wankhede Stadium, in Mumbai.
Philanthropy: After retirement, Tendulkar has been involved in various charitable activities and philanthropic efforts, focusing on education and healthcare for underprivileged children.
Public Life: He has also been a prominent public figure and has taken on roles such as the Member of Parliament for the Rajya Sabha from 2012 to 2018.
Legacy:
Tendulkar is often referred to as the "Little Master" or "Master Blaster" and is celebrated globally for his contributions to cricket. His impact on the game and his status as a sporting icon continue to inspire cricketers and fans around the world.
